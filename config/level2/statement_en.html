Welcome to your second lesson

You will learn how to use if-else conditions

  The Goal

After the first round you are strong enough to withstand many spell attacks. But your strength is limited. If the Maester Wizard starts magic many spells, you must attack too. If the Wizard’s spell power is greater than 100, you must start attacking as well.

  Rules

From the beginning of the game you will resist a large number of spells. You can defend against them (using DEFEND spell) if their magic power is above 100. If the magic power of Wizard spells is weaker, you have to use your own attack spell (using ATTACK spell)

Be careful! You must save your attack spells. Use them only, if it is necessary.
 
Victory Conditions
  • You win if the magic power of Maester Wizard’s spell is less than or equal to 100 and you use ATTACK spell.
  • You win if the magic power of Maester Wizard’s spell is greater than 100 and you use DEFEND spell.
 
Lose Conditions
  • You lose if the magic power of Maester Wizard spell is less than or equal to 100 and you won't use ATTACK spell.
  • You lose if the magic power of Maester Wizard spell is more than 100 and you won't use DEFEND spell.

  Game Input

In each turn, your program must read single text from standard input and based on provided rules print either ATTACK (if input is <= 100) or DEFEND (if input is > 100). Don't forget that your output has to end with a newline
Input

Single line: spell , the Maester wizard is casting against you

Output
A single line of text. Either ATTACK or DEFEND based on game rules
